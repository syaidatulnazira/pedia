World Wide Fund for Nature

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF) is an international non-governmental organization working in the field of wilderness preservation and the reduction of human impact on the environment.

History

WWF was founded in 1961 and has grown to become one of the world’s largest and most effective conservation organizations.

Mission

WWF’s mission is to stop the degradation of the planet’s natural environment and to build a future in which humans live in harmony with nature.

Activities

  • Wildlife conservation
  • Climate change action
  • Forest and ocean protection
  • Sustainable development
